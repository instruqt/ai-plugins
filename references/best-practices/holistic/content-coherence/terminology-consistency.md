# Terminology Consistency

The same concept should use the same term throughout the track.

## Criteria

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Poor | The same concept is referred to by multiple different terms across the track with no acknowledgment. Users will be confused about whether different terms mean different things. |
| 2 | Below Standard | Terminology is mostly consistent but switches without explanation in two or more places. Some terms are used interchangeably without establishing the relationship. |
| 3 | Adequate | Core terminology is consistent. One or two instances of alternative terms appear without explicit introduction, but the meaning is still clear from context. |
| 4 | Good | Terms are consistent throughout. Where synonyms exist, they are introduced together (e.g., "staging area, also called the index"). Product-specific terms match the style guide. Minor slip possible. |
| 5 | Excellent | Every concept uses a single primary term consistently. All synonyms and alternative names are explicitly introduced at first use and then used deliberately. No unexplained term switching anywhere in the track. |

- Don't switch between "staging area" and "index" without establishing both terms
- Don't call it "the remote" in one place and "origin" in another without explanation
- Product-specific terms should match the customer's style guide
- Ensure consistent terminology across assignment.md files and check/solve scripts

## What to Watch For

- Inconsistent terminology for the same concept across challenges
