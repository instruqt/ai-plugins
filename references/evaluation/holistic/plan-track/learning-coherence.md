# Learning Coherence

A single gestalt criterion: does the track plan tell a coherent learning story? This rubric evaluates the track plan as a whole -- not individual components, but whether objectives, audience, challenge roadmap, and infrastructure design form a unified, purposeful design. A track plan can score well on every analytic criterion individually yet still feel disjointed when read as a whole.

## Overall coherence

Evaluate whether the track plan reads as a unified learning design where all parts reinforce each other.

Consider: Do the learning objectives match the audience level? Does the challenge roadmap build toward those objectives? Does the infrastructure support the roadmap? Does every element serve the same learning story?

Score 4: Objectives, audience, roadmap, and infrastructure form a coherent design. Each section reinforces the others. A reader finishes the plan with a clear picture of what the track teaches and how. No section feels disconnected.
Score 5: The plan reads as a deliberate, unified learning narrative. Every element serves the same story. The roadmap is the natural consequence of the objectives, the infrastructure is the natural consequence of the roadmap, and the audience definition calibrates every decision. The plan could be handed to any implementer and produce the same track.
Score 3: Most sections align, but one section feels disconnected from the rest -- the infrastructure is over-specified for the objectives, or the roadmap does not fully deliver on the stated objectives.
Score 2: Objectives and roadmap point in different directions. The plan has internal contradictions -- stated goals do not match the planned challenges, or the audience definition does not match the complexity of the roadmap.
Score 1: The plan is a collection of independently written sections with no unifying purpose. Objectives, audience, roadmap, and infrastructure could belong to different tracks.
