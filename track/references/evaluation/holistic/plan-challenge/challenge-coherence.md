# Challenge Coherence

A single gestalt criterion: does the challenge plan build naturally on prior work and form a coherent challenge? This rubric evaluates the challenge plan as a whole -- not individual steps or scripts, but whether the challenge has a unified purpose, builds logically on what came before, and prepares the learner for what comes next.

## Overall coherence

Evaluate whether the challenge plan reads as a purposeful, self-contained learning unit that fits within the larger track narrative.

Consider: Does the challenge have a clear learning goal? Do the planned assignment steps build toward that goal? Do check assertions validate the concepts being taught? Do infrastructure changes support the challenge's needs? Does the challenge connect naturally to prior and subsequent challenges?

Score 4: Assignment steps, check assertions, and infrastructure changes form a coherent challenge with a clear purpose. The challenge builds naturally on prior challenges and prepares for subsequent ones. A reader finishes the plan knowing exactly what the challenge accomplishes.
Score 5: The challenge plan is a tight, purposeful unit. Every assignment step serves the challenge goal. Check assertions validate at exactly the right moments. Infrastructure changes are precisely what the challenge needs and nothing more. The challenge could not be improved by reordering, adding, or removing steps. It fits seamlessly in the track's learning arc.
Score 3: The challenge has a recognizable purpose and mostly builds on prior work, but one element feels out of place -- a step that does not serve the challenge goal, a check assertion that tests a different concept, or an infrastructure change that seems unrelated.
Score 2: The challenge has a vague purpose and weak connection to surrounding challenges. Steps are loosely related but do not build toward a clear outcome. The challenge feels like a collection of related topics rather than a designed learning experience.
Score 1: The challenge plan has no unifying purpose. Steps, check assertions, and infrastructure changes are unrelated. The challenge does not build on prior challenges or prepare for subsequent ones. It could be removed from the track without affecting the learning narrative.
