# Audience Definition

Evaluates whether the track plan defines its target audience with enough specificity that a content author could make independent decisions about explanation depth, terminology, and scaffolding. A vague audience definition ("beginners") forces every downstream decision to be a guess.

## Audience definition

Evaluate whether the plan specifies skill level, prerequisites, and assumed knowledge clearly enough to guide content authoring decisions.

Score 4: Clear skill level, specific prerequisites (named skills or tools, not just "familiarity with"), and assumed knowledge documented. A content author could independently judge explanation depth.
Score 5: Audience definition is precise enough to resolve edge cases -- for example, whether to explain what a container is or assume the learner already knows. Prerequisite boundaries are explicit: "assumes X, does not assume Y."
Score 3: Skill level and prerequisites are listed but vague or overly broad (e.g., "some experience with Linux"). Not enough detail to guide content depth decisions.
Score 2: Only skill level mentioned (e.g., "beginners") with no prerequisites or context about what the audience already knows.
Score 1: No audience defined. Content authors would have no basis for calibrating explanation depth.

## No assumed knowledge beyond prerequisites

Evaluate whether the plan's content scope stays within the boundaries set by the prerequisites -- nothing is planned that requires knowledge not listed.

Score 4: All planned content stays within the prerequisite boundary. New concepts are explicitly identified as things the track will teach, not assume.
Score 5: Prerequisites are comprehensive and verifiable. Every concept the track uses is either listed as a prerequisite or explicitly planned as a learning objective. The boundary is airtight.
Score 3: Most content stays within prerequisites, but one or two planned topics assume knowledge beyond what is listed.
Score 2: Several planned topics assume skills or knowledge not listed in prerequisites.
Score 1: Repeatedly plans to use concepts not listed in prerequisites. A learner meeting only the stated prereqs would be lost.
