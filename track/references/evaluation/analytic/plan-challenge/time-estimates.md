# Time Estimates

Evaluates whether time estimates in the challenge plan are realistic for the planned content. Unrealistic estimates lead to tracks that frustrate learners (too short) or bore them (too long). Good estimates account for reading time, hands-on time, and the time learners spend troubleshooting when things go wrong.

## Per-challenge time realism

Evaluate whether the estimated duration for this challenge is realistic given its content.

Score 4: Estimate accounts for reading the assignment, performing hands-on steps, and a buffer for mistakes. Challenges typically fall in the 5-15 minute range, with complex challenges up to 20 minutes.
Score 5: Estimate is precise and defensible. It reflects the specific steps planned, the target audience's skill level (a beginner takes longer than an expert), and includes implicit buffer for troubleshooting.
Score 3: Estimate is in the right ballpark but may be slightly optimistic — actual completion would take 1.5x the estimate for the target audience.
Score 2: Estimate exists but is consistently too optimistic — actual completion would take 2-3x longer, especially for the stated audience level.
Score 1: No time estimate, or wildly unrealistic (e.g., 2 minutes for a multi-step debugging exercise, or 30 minutes for a single command).

## Cumulative track time coherence

Evaluate whether this challenge's time estimate fits within the overall track timing budget.

Score 4: The challenge's estimate, combined with other planned challenges, sums to a total that fits within the track's timelimit with a 20-30% buffer.
Score 5: The estimate is precisely calibrated to leave appropriate buffer. The track's total estimated duration accounts for setup time, inter-challenge transitions, and reading notes slides.
Score 3: The estimate fits within the track total but the buffer is tight (less than 15%) or generous (more than 50%).
Score 2: The cumulative time is close to or exceeds the track's timelimit, leaving no buffer for slower learners.
Score 1: The cumulative time far exceeds the track's timelimit, or time estimates are missing for this challenge.

## Complexity-duration alignment

Evaluate whether the time estimate matches the challenge's stated difficulty and the number of steps planned.

Score 4: Basic challenges are estimated at 5-8 minutes, intermediate at 8-15 minutes, advanced at 12-20 minutes. The estimate is proportional to the number of steps and the complexity of each step.
Score 5: Duration precisely reflects complexity. A challenge with one simple command is short. A challenge requiring debugging or multi-step configuration is longer. The difficulty label, step count, and time estimate are all coherent.
Score 3: Estimate roughly matches complexity but is noticeably off for the difficulty level — an advanced challenge estimated at 5 minutes, or a basic challenge at 20 minutes.
Score 2: Estimate does not match complexity. Simple challenges have inflated estimates or complex challenges have compressed ones.
Score 1: No relationship between complexity and estimated duration.
