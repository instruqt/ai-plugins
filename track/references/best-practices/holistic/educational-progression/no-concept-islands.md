# No Concept Islands

Every concept should connect to something before and after it. Avoid teaching concepts in isolation that never connect to the rest of the track.

## Criteria

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Poor | Multiple concepts are taught in isolation and never referenced again. The track reads like a collection of unrelated tutorials. |
| 2 | Below Standard | Most concepts connect to each other, but one or two are introduced and then dropped entirely. No bridging language connects them to the rest of the track. |
| 3 | Adequate | Concepts generally connect forward and backward. One concept feels isolated — it is taught but never built upon or referenced in later challenges. |
| 4 | Good | All concepts connect to the broader track. Bridging language is present in most transitions. Concepts build on each other and compound over challenges. One connection could be stronger. |
| 5 | Excellent | Every concept is woven into the track narrative. Bridging language explicitly connects each new concept to what came before and what comes next. No concept is introduced and abandoned. Skills compound naturally across challenges. |

Use bridging language to make connections explicit:
- "In the previous challenge, you learned X. Now we'll build on that with Y."
- "This will become important when we cover Z in the next challenge."

## What to Watch For

- A concept that is taught once and never referenced or built upon again
- Challenges that feel like standalone tutorials with no connection to the track narrative
- Missing bridging language between challenges that cover related concepts
