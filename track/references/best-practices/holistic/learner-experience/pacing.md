# Pacing

Challenges should be consistently paced to keep learners engaged without fatigue -- evaluates cross-challenge rhythm and timing balance.

## Criteria

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Poor | Challenge durations vary wildly -- some take under a minute, others over 20 minutes. No breaks between intense sections. No summary or consolidation challenges. |
| 2 | Below Standard | Most challenges are within a reasonable time range, but two or more challenges are significantly too long or too short. Pacing feels uneven across the track. |
| 3 | Adequate | Challenges generally take 3-10 minutes each. One challenge exceeds 15 minutes or feels rushed. Summary challenges exist but are minimal. |
| 4 | Good | Challenges are consistently paced at 3-10 minutes. Hands-on and reading sections alternate well. Summary challenges consolidate learning. One minor pacing issue. |
| 5 | Excellent | Every challenge is well-paced at 3-10 minutes. Intense hands-on sections are followed by lighter reading or summary sections. Conclusion challenges consolidate learning effectively. The overall rhythm keeps learners engaged without fatigue. |

- Each challenge should take roughly the same amount of time (3-10 minutes)
- No single challenge should take more than 15 minutes
- Conclusion/summary challenges help users consolidate learning
- Total track duration should generally stay within 30-60 minutes. Longer tracks risk fatigue and drop-off — consider splitting into a multi-track learning path instead.

## What to Watch For

- Challenges that take wildly different amounts of time
- Tracks that exceed 60 minutes total — consider whether they should be split
