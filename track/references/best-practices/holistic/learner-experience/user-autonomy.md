# User Autonomy

The amount of written context, troubleshooting notes, and reflection prompts should match the level of user autonomy -- evaluated across all challenges for consistency with the track's delivery context.

## Criteria

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Poor | Guidance level is badly mismatched to the delivery context. A track intended for independent use has sparse instructions and no troubleshooting notes. A track for guided sessions has walls of text that duplicate what an instructor would say. |
| 2 | Below Standard | Guidance level partially fits but has significant gaps -- e.g., an independent track missing troubleshooting notes at key confusion points, or a guided track with lengthy setup that wastes instructor time. |
| 3 | Adequate | Guidance level generally fits the delivery context. Minor mismatches exist -- one challenge could use more notes for independent learners, or one section is overwritten for a guided setting. |
| 4 | Good | Guidance level is well-calibrated. Independent sections have thorough context and callouts. Guided sections are activity-focused. One minor adjustment would improve the fit. |
| 5 | Excellent | Guidance level is precisely calibrated throughout. Every section provides exactly the amount of context its learners need -- no more, no less. Independent learners never feel abandoned. Supported learners never feel lectured at. |

Tracks exist on a spectrum from fully instructor-supported to fully independent. The key question is: if the learner gets confused at this point, is there a human available to help? Where the answer is no, the content itself must provide that support.

**More supported** (instructor present, guided workshops):
- Shorter written instructions -- the instructor fills gaps verbally
- Faster challenge flow with less reading between activities

**More independent** (self-paced, async, no instructor):
- Fuller written explanations and context at each step
- More notes, tips, and troubleshooting callouts anticipating where learners get stuck
- Knowledge checks or reflection prompts after challenges to confirm understanding

## What to Watch For

- Independent tracks with sparse instructions and no troubleshooting notes
- Guided tracks with walls of text that duplicate what an instructor would say
- Same level of written detail regardless of delivery context
