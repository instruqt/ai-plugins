# No Assumed Knowledge Beyond Prerequisites

If the description says "No prior experience required", the content must not use terminology, tools, or concepts beyond the stated prerequisites without explaining them first -- evaluated across all challenges.

## Criteria

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Poor | The content repeatedly assumes knowledge that the prerequisites explicitly say is not required. Users without that knowledge would be lost within the first few challenges. |
| 2 | Below Standard | Several concepts are used without explanation despite being outside the stated prerequisites. A user meeting only the listed prerequisites would struggle in multiple places. |
| 3 | Adequate | Most content stays within the prerequisite boundary. One or two concepts slip through without adequate introduction, but a motivated learner could figure them out from context. |
| 4 | Good | Content stays within the stated prerequisites. All significant concepts are introduced. One minor assumption exists that slightly exceeds the prerequisite boundary. |
| 5 | Excellent | The content strictly honors the stated prerequisites. Every concept outside the prerequisite boundary is introduced and explained before use. A learner meeting exactly the listed prerequisites would never be lost. |

Content must not:
- Use terminology without explaining it first
- Assume users know what foundational concepts are without introduction
- Skip over fundamental concepts
- Reference tools or workflows without introduction

## What to Watch For

- Content that assumes knowledge the prerequisites say isn't needed
- Technical terms used before they're introduced
