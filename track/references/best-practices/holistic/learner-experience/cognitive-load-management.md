# Cognitive Load Management

Evaluates whether the track avoids overwhelming users with too much information at once, across all challenges.

## Criteria

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Poor | Multiple concepts are introduced simultaneously with no practice in between. Assignments are walls of text. Theory is front-loaded with practice deferred to much later. |
| 2 | Below Standard | Concepts are mostly introduced one at a time, but several challenges introduce two or more new ideas before the user gets to practice any of them. Some assignments are text-heavy. |
| 3 | Adequate | Each challenge generally focuses on one concept with practice following. One or two challenges pack too much information or delay practice. Progressive disclosure is partially used. |
| 4 | Good | Concepts are introduced one at a time with practice immediately following. Progressive disclosure is used effectively. One challenge could benefit from being split or restructured. |
| 5 | Excellent | Every concept is introduced individually and immediately practiced. Theory and practice alternate naturally. Progressive disclosure is used throughout -- basic usage first, options and details later. No challenge overwhelms the learner. |

- Introduce one concept at a time
- Let users practice before introducing the next concept
- Use progressive disclosure (basic first, details later)
- Don't front-load all the theory before any practice
- Each challenge should teach one main concept, validated by its check script — prefer many smaller challenges over fewer large ones that test multiple concepts at once

## What to Watch For

- Challenges that dump large amounts of theory with no practice
- A single challenge trying to teach and validate multiple unrelated concepts — split it
